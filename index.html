About Me

Hello, my name is David Mattia and I am a Computer Science Major at the University of Notre Dame. My two main interests are big data and systems computing (especially if they are used in some sort of social work or medicine), but I enjoy dabbling with every aspect of computer science. I am currently an intern at General Electric and am creating algorithms to detect potential engine issues before they become real problems. Let me know if you want to chat!

I am a Community Leader

I look to find simple ways to help out those around me. I have found my calling as a blood drive coordinator for the American Red Cross and love how easy it is to make a large impact. I have hosted over eight drives and am now broadening my focus to work with military and veteran relief through the Red Cross. I also love working with disability networks. I have been a camp counselor for the Muscular Dystrophy Association for two years and am the web master for the GE Disabilities Network. If you know of any events I can help with, please let me know and I'll be glad to help.

Community Member

I am blessed with opportunity at Notre Dame, and am trying to make the most out of it. I belong to the large community of Notre Dame, but also to smaller groups such as being a member of Stanford Hall, the Computer Science and Engineering Department, the American Red Cross Club, the Bengal Bouts Boxing Program, the 1 E/W football team, and many others. In each group, I strive to build up those around me. I have always been taught that a true leader does not need a leadership position, but can strengthen a system from anywhere in an organization. I look to be a good member by personally working as hard as I can, encouraging others, asking questions when I am not sure the best way forward, and maintaining a positive attitude.

Projects

Sheep Herder

View on Github

For my final project sophomore year, I created a herd movement algorithm and simulation. The simulation is comprised of multiple sheep trying to form a herd around a user specified point. The sheep avoid each other and obstacles such as fences and stones to surround their goal.

MathFeud

View online

I created this website alongside Professor Zhang for a research project. The purpose of the project is to collect data on how 8th-9th grade math students process data while doing math problems. This project is a work in progress. The gitlab repo is private, so contact me to view source code.

Foober

View on Github

Created at IrishHacks, this native android app connects Notre Dame students who are looking to deliver/buy food. Deliverers could charge a small fee, and would accept requests to bring food to users. All food requests were stored in a Parse database and communication between the two parties was handled via SMS. This app won 3rd place overall, and I ported it to native iPhone since then.

Chiellini

View on Github

A pacman variant created using C and the Xlib graphics library. You play as Giorgio Chiellini trying to avoid being bitten by Luis Suarez. This was my first semester final project as a sophomore.

Pebble Watchfaces

View on Github

I have created many Pebble watchfaces using a variety of apis. I created the faces natively with C and JavaScript. This example was a personal watchface I created for the company I am interning for, GE. It relies on two separate apis: google finance for stock information and yahoo weather for the current temperature.
